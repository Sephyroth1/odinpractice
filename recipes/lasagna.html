lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that has become popular worldwide. It is a layered pasta casserole dish, known for its rich and hearty flavor. The traditional preparation of lasagna involves stacking layers of flat, wide pasta noodles with various ingredients

ingredients

  • Meat Sauce
  • cheese filling
  • bechamel sauce

instructions

  1. In a large skillet, brown the ground meat over medium heat. Add onions and garlic and sauté until softened.
  2. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste, red wine (if using), oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer the sauce for about 30 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  3. In a bowl, combine ricotta, mozzarella, Parmesan, egg, parsley, salt, and pepper to create the cheese filling mixture.
  4. If using bechamel sauce, melt butter in a saucepan, whisk in flour, and then gradually add milk, stirring continuously until the sauce thickens. Season with salt and nutmeg.
  5. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions.
  6. Assemble the lasagna in a baking dish by layering noodles, meat sauce, cheese filling, and optional bechamel sauce.
  7. Repeat the layers until the dish is filled, finishing with a layer of meat sauce. Top with shredded mozzarella and additional grated Parmesan.
  8. Bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for about 25-30 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly, and the edges are golden brown.
  9. Allow the lasagna to cool for a few minutes before serving.
